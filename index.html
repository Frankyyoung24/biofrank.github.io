Some thing about the moRNA

miRNA-offset RNAs, also termed moRNAs or moRs, are the additional products to miRNAs. This new class of RNAs was first reported by Shi et al. in the sea squirt Ciona intestinalis, and were recently mentioBned in several deep sequencing technology (RNA-seq) data sets.

As a novel type small RNA, moRNAs are ~20 nt long and derived from sequences contiguously adjacent to the ~60 nt pre-miRNAs. Their particular location suggests that moRNAs are likely produced by RNAse III-like processing from the pre-miRNA hairpin, as the co-products from Drosha cleavage of the pre-miRNA. In general, moRNAs are expressed in relatively less abundance compared to mature miRNAs and show greater 5’ heterogeneity. Previous screening studies indicated that they are developmentally regulated and have a bias to arise upstream of the loci by virtue of a rigid miRNA’s 5’-terminal identity. Several abundantly expressed moRNAs were proved to localize in the nucleus while most of the mature miRNAs are found in the cytoplasm. Castanotto et al. suggested that the cytoplasm-nucleus transportation of particular moRNAs may be similar to some miRNAs that show specific nuclear enrichment. But some data suggests this may be due to a specific nuclear small RNA synthesis-enzymes-like processing.
